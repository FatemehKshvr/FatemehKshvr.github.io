Advances in DSR: a Contrastive Learning Approach with XlS-R Representations for Speaker Identity Preservation

Authors: Fatemeh Keshvari, Rahil Mahdian Toroghi, Hassan Zareian
Comments: Submitted to ICASSP 2024.

Abstract: Dysarthric Speech Reconstruction (DSR) is challenging due to its pathological sound patterns. Preserving speaker identity, especially without access to normal speech, is a key challenge. Our proposed approach uses contrastive learning to extract speaker embeddings for reconstruction, while employing XLS-R representations instead of filter banks. The results show improved speech quality, naturalness, intelligibility, speaker identity preservation, and gender consistency for female speakers. Reconstructed speech exhibits 1.51 and 2.12 MOS score improvements and reduces word error rates by 25.45% and 32.1% for moderate and moderate-severe dysarthria speakers, respectively. This approach offers promising advancements in dysarthric speech reconstruction.
